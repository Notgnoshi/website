My school offers two options for their masters programs: thesis, or project. I originally planned on completing a thesis, but soon came to the realization that I would not finish in time. I'm burned out, and want to be done with school, so I decided to switch tracks and move the goalposts wherever the hell I need to in order to graduate. I'm not going into academia, and I have a job offer contingent on my graduation; I'm getting out.

The different between a thesis and a project is that a project is an implementation of something, and not necessarily original research. There's no research question, and no massive thesis that needs writing, defense, approval, and publishing shenanigans through the graduate office.

My deliverable is a commandline application to generate haiku using one of several offered language models. Unfortunately, that means much of the time I've spent on much of the content here was wasted.
